About Me

I'm currently a 3rd year PhD Student in Engineering and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon, I study the labor and community implications of the energy transition to ease the friction of becoming carbon neutral

Over the past 10 years I have worked across several industries, including iron & steelmaking, EV battery manufacturing, and coal production. Additionally I have worked with several state and federal agencies in my research and professional career, including the following: PA DCED, TSA, IRS, CDC/ASPR, FEMA, and Dept of State.

I do my best to stay creative and enjoy pursuits outside of research, including dancing & choreographing, stained glass, writing, and following niche sports.
